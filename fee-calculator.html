üìö The Truth About Etsy Fees

One of the biggest shock new sellers face is seeing how much Etsy actually takes from each sale. It's easy to think "I sold something for $50, so I made $50!" But that's not how it works.

Etsy's fee structure is multi-layered: You pay a listing fee upfront ($0.20), a transaction fee when it sells (6.5% of the TOTAL including shipping), payment processing fees (3% + $0.25), and potentially advertising fees if you use Etsy Ads or if Offsite Ads drives the sale.

For a $50 sale with $5 shipping, you're not keeping $55. After fees, you're keeping around $50.50. That's $4.50 gone‚Äî8.2% of your revenue‚Äîbefore you even account for your actual costs.

This calculator shows you the real numbers. No surprises, no shock when you get paid. You'll see exactly what Etsy takes, what you actually receive, and you can plan your pricing accordingly.

üí° Understanding Etsy Fees

Listing Fee ($0.20): Charged when you create a listing. Renews every 4 months if unsold, or immediately after it sells.

Transaction Fee (6.5%): Etsy's commission on your sale. Applied to the TOTAL (item + shipping).

Payment Processing (3% + $0.25): Credit card processing fees. Every payment platform charges something similar.

Etsy Ads: Optional. You set a daily budget. Only pay the 15% fee when an ad leads to a sale.

Offsite Ads: Etsy advertises your products on Google/Facebook. You pay 12-15% only if the ad drives a sale. Mandatory if you make $10k+/year.
